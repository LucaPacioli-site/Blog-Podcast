A Story That Started With "Hello"

It all started with Charles. Charles is a college student. He described himself as someone who likes computers, anime, and other weird stuff. He plays some sports, but he still sees himself as a nobody. Charles is someone who likes being alone, yet he hates the thing called loneliness. Being an introvert, he admits that making a conversation with someone makes his heart pound, and being alone enables him to do some things without being judged. In short, socializing was really hard for him.

One morning while doing his work in the hallway, someone suddenly appeared right in front of him and said "Hello!". He wasn't so shocked that time, but one thing he knew was that that word changed something to him. That someone was a girl named Angelica. She came to Charles and asked him for lunch. Charles, who's very shy to people, without hesitation, agreed to have lunch with her. He felt anxious, but hides his fear with a smile anyways. He never expected someone to approach him just like that. She kept talking to him like nothing, and he replied like he never feared talking.

Day by day, they got to know each other more. Charles became friends with Angelica's friends. Some have vices, some are gifted and some are broken, but he learned to accept them. To him, minutes became hours, laughter became memories and everyday became fun. He still loves being alone, but what changed is that loneliness doesn't hit him anymore. That's when he realized that it's not hard to make new friends, to be accepted and to accept others. What's hard is having fear that stops you from doing things.

What Does Understanding of Human Personality Mean in the Short Film "Hello"?

Due to the diverse personalities of people, like Charles, we develop a fear of interacting with others because we are afraid of being judged for our actions. Despite this, there are cases where it does not develop, but rather it is actually part of an individual's personality. There are also people like Angelica, who have an open understanding of different types of people and therefore able to interact with them.

We don't need to change ourselves just to fit in the society, because we are the society. Having a broad perspective on the personality of others can help us understand some of the reasons for their actions. This is inherent in our human nature, which is why we should not force on them what is right in our perception, but rather it is more important that we accept our differences and embrace the reality of who we are as humans.

The story of Charles is not a man-made story or any fantasy that we're used to encountering. His story is just a reflection of humans like him. But in reality, we're not all the same with Charles. "A girl named Ysa was invited by her friends to hang out this Saturday. Her friends accepted her excuse and continued their hang out. Knowing that without her, they still continue their hang out and that didn't bother Ysa because she understands that her friends are very outgoing and just want to spend time together.
